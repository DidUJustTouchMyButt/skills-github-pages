Why Switch to AI Studio?

AI Studio (formerly MakerSuite) is a professional workbench. It isn't just a "chat"; it's where you build things. Here are the tools you get here that you don't get in the normal app:

1. The "System Instruction"

In the standard app, you just start typing. In AI Studio, there is a special box called System Instructions. This is where you set the "God Mode" rules.

  • Standard App: You have to constantly remind it "Be concise" or "Don't use emojis."
  • AI Studio: You write "You are a cynical noir detective. Never use emojis. Keep answers under 50 words" in the system box once, and it obeys that persona perfectly for the entire session.

2. Control the "Temperature"

AI Studio has sliders on the right side. The most important is Temperature.

  • Low Temperature (0.0 - 0.2): Makes the AI rigorous and factual. Essential for math, coding, or summarizing legal PDFs.
  • High Temperature (0.8 - 1.0): Makes the AI creative and "dreamy." Perfect for brainstorming.

3. The New Brains: Gemini 3 Pro

This is the big difference. In Studio, you can manually select the model:

  • Gemini 3 Pro (The Beast): The newest model. It features "Deep Think" capabilities for complex reasoning and "Vibe Coding" for building software.
  • Gemini 2.5 Flash: Extremely fast and cheap. Use this for quick summaries of long documents.

4. Save & Reuse Prompts

In AI Studio, you save your setups as "Prompts" (like files). If you build a perfect "Contract Analyzer" setup, you save it. Next time, just open that file, drop in a new PDF, and run it. The standard app treats everything as a fleeting chat history.

Everyday Uses for LLMs

Even if you aren't doing technical work, these models are incredible productivity multipliers for daily life.

  • Drafting Difficult Emails: Paste a rude email you received. Ask the AI: "Write a polite but firm professional response that declines their request without burning bridges."
  • Meal Planning (The "Fridge" trick): Take a photo of the inside of your refrigerator. Upload it to Gemini. Ask: "Based on these ingredients, give me 3 dinner recipes I can make right now."
  • YouTube Summaries: Don't have time for a 45-minute tutorial? Paste the YouTube link into Gemini 3 Pro and ask: "Give me a bulleted list of the key takeaways and any tools mentioned in this video."
  • Complex Explainers: Ask for analogies. "Explain how a heat pump works to a 5-year-old using a Minecraft analogy."
